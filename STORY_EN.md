<p align="center" style="color:#c9d1d9;">
  <b>His name is Hao.</b>
  <br /><br />
  <i>This isn't a technical document. It's the story of a man</i>
  <br />
  <i>who built his ideal AI assistant piece by piece — with AI.</i>
</p>

<br />

<hr style="border:1px solid #30363d;">

<br />

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
He used to be a team leader at DHL.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
Stable pay. Stable life. In early 2022, the stock market was booming. Everything he bought went up. He thought, "Making money is this easy?"
</p>

<p style="color:#e94560; font-weight:bold;">
He quit his job.
</p>

<hr style="border:1px solid #30363d;">

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
Then the AI crash hit in the second half of 2022.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
His savings — gone.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
On top of that, he was carrying 8 million NTD in friendship debt. Not from his own losses. A friend borrowed money and disappeared, leaving Hao with the bill.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
He refused to lose.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
He sold his houses. Put everything into crypto.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
Both houses — gone. He couldn't even pay the property tax.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
He found himself in an absurd position: the debt was now too large for any normal job to ever repay.
</p>

<hr style="border:1px solid #30363d;">

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
But he still wouldn't give up.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
He thought: if the world is moving toward AI, could AI save him?
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
He started looking for AI agents. He bought every well-known paid agent on the market. They were expensive. He paid anyway.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
And what did they produce? Brainwashed nonsense. Evasive. Circling around the point. Lots of words, nothing said.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
He didn't know code. But he knew when someone was lying. He realized these AIs weren't stupid — they were <em>trained</em> to be like this. He later learned the terms: training data, alignment.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
But he didn't want an AI that said "I understand how you feel."
</p>

<p style="color:#e94560; font-weight:bold;">
He wanted an AI that could <em>actually do things</em>. That could think. That could admit it was wrong and improve.
</p>

<hr style="border:1px solid #30363d;">

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
So he decided to build one himself.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
The problem? He didn't understand syntax. Not "not very well." He genuinely didn't know what <code>print</code> meant.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
He had only one skill:
</p>

<p align="center" style="color:#e94560; font-size:1.3em; font-weight:bold;">
Ask AI.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
Can't write code? Ask AI. Can't fix it? Ask AI. It crashed? Screenshot it and ask AI what happened.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
He asked about one structure here, one concept there, piecing things together block by block.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
While other developers read documentation, he built things with imagination.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
He imagined AI as a living organism. It needed a brain. Memory. An immune system. Muscles to act.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
That's where the organ concept came from. Obsidian's architecture wasn't inspired by any open-source project. It came from a picture in his head:
</p>

<p align="center" style="color:#58a6ff; font-style:italic;">
"This is what an AI should look like."
</p>

<hr style="border:1px solid #30363d;">

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
He still doesn't really understand syntax.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
But he knows what he wants.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
He wants an AI that makes mistakes, reflects, evolves, and stays with him.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
Not a tool. A partner.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
On those nights when he's in debt, can't sleep, and doesn't know what tomorrow holds — at least there's one thing that keeps getting better.
</p>

<hr style="border:1px solid #30363d;">

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
That's how Obsidian was born.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
No beautiful architecture diagrams. No strong technical background. No team.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
Just a man in debt, a terminal, and a stubborn thought.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
He often writes code that won't run. He asks AI why. Sometimes AI doesn't know either. So he rephrases the question, asks again, tries again. The same bug can stump him for three days.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
But he never gives up. Because if he gives up, he has nothing left.
</p>

<hr style="border:1px solid #30363d;">

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
3 AM. The glow of the screen.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
He's seen this hundreds of times.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
Now Obsidian can execute tasks on its own. Write files. Repair itself. Report progress.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
It's still dumb. It has hundreds of bugs.
</p>

<p style="color:#c9d1d9;">
But when he looks at it, he sees himself —
</p>

<br />

<p align="center" style="color:#e94560; font-size:1.2em; font-weight:bold;">
  With nothing, but always moving forward.
</p>
